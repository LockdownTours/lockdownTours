Nonebei Yokocho

A lot of people after a busy day in Shibuya want to head back to the hotel but that would be a mistake! For a taste of the Tokyo spirit, I highly recommend visiting Nonebei Yokocho (known as Drunkard’s Alley). Reminiscent of Shinjuku’s Golden Gia, it's one of the more local experiences you can have in a neighbourhood that has been steamrolled by tourists from all over the world.

With courtesy of Chris Yang (unsplash)

With courtesy of Lan Pham (unsplash)

The tiny alleyways are perfect for capturing the "Tokyo mood". Most places have red lanterns outside which are illuminated during the night. Everything in Yokocho differs widely from other countries, from the intimate architecture and eccentric aesthetics. Take the Japanese-style standing bar, where the bartenders have more room to move than the customers!

Nowadays most of the bars welcome tourists, but some still abide by a "locals only" policy. A good rule of thumb is to notice if there is any English posted out front. If so, you're probably in the clear. If standing for long periods of time in noisy, tiny rooms doesn’t sound like your cup of sake there are other tiny (think four to five people) bars, restaurants and cafes to patron at. Some of these bars have been in Yokocho for more than 70 years! It's a brilliant place to grab a bite or to relax with a whiskey before heading out to the 24-hour karaoke round the corner.
